Overview

My team and I worked with One Iota Golf Performance to identify opportunities to better address their users' needs, through various research methods and concept iterations. The final deliverable was a product proposal for the "One Iota Training Library", complete with an interactive mockup and concept video.

This project was completed as part of a senior level interaction design course at Simon Fraser University. This project was also featured in the School of Interactive Arts + Technology Fall 2023 Showcase.

Concept Video

Design Focus

In our first meeting, our client explained that customers were facing multiple issues with the website, interferring with their ability to place orders.

To better understand customers' pain points, our team decided to evaluate the design and structure of the online ordering process for home delivery and in-store pickup to identify sources of friction and areas for improvement.

Heuristic Evaluation

METHODOLOGY

First, I conducted a heuristic evaluation of the online ordering process using Jakob Nielsen’s 10 interaction design principles. For each issue identified, I noted which heuristic was violated, the severity of the issue, and potential solutions. Issues with a severity rating of 3 or 4 were more critical as they hindered the user’s ability to complete tasks and were therefore a higher priority. These ratings later guided our redesign process as they allowed our team to address critical issues first, given the time constraints of our project.

Walkthrough of the existing Bonus Bakery's website

FINDINGS

I identified seven key issues in the ordering process, two of which had a severity rating of 4 and are discussed in more detail below. My complete heuristic evaluation report can be found here.

Issue 1: Cumbersome flavour selection process

Users need to manually type their selected flavours in a text box. However, the flavour options are listed near the bottom of the page, so users must either remember which flavours they want or scroll up and down the page to refer to the list.

HEURISTIC BROKEN

Recognition rather than recall

SEVERITY RATING

4

RECOMMENDATION

Replace the text box with a list of flavour options and add a quantity selector next to each item. This would clearly highlight the available options and simplify the input process.

Issue 2: Unclear deadline and delivery dates

The current delivery date selection menu does not take the bakery’s order deadlines into account, so users can select a pickup / delivery option even after the deadline has passed. There is also no mention of the order deadlines on the product pages, so the user must remember this information from the initial pop-up on the home page.

HEURISTIC BROKEN

Error prevention, Recognition rather than recall

SEVERITY RATING

4

RECOMMENDATION

Include the order deadline information on each product page or as a thin banner at the top of the page. If possible, only display pickup / delivery options that are available for that day.

Usability Testing

METHODOLOGY

To gain further insight into the issues identified in the heuristic evaluation, I conducted think-aloud usability tests with 5 participants, including current and prospective Bonus Bakery customers.

Participants were given three tasks and were asked to describe their thoughts out loud as they completed them. Each task required participants to identify a product that fulfilled the needs of a given scenario and to go through the ordering process until they reached the checkout page.

FINDINGS

"I wish that somewhere before choosing the selection, it would tell me the amount of business days it would take for it to be ready."

- Participant 2, Prospective Customer

"It would be nice if it mentioned somewhere that there are no deliveries or pickups on Sundays."

- Participant 4, Current Customer

A critical concern that participants expressed was that the deadline and delivery information could not be easily found. This aligned with the second issue found in the heuristic evaluation. Although this information was presented in an initial pop-up on the homepage, many participants had difficulty recalling this information when it came to selecting their desired delivery date.

Prototype

Based on the findings from the heuristic evaluation and usability studies, our team created an interactive prototype with recommended changes.

One notable change was the addition of static cards on the homepage with the delivery and deadline information.

Reflection

This project provided an excellent opportunity to explore a variety of UX research methodologies and develop my skills in designing and conducting usability studies. Analyzing, synthesizing, and integrating research findings into data-driven design solutions was another key aspect of my learning experience.

One significant takeaway was the critical role of participant selection in ensuring the reliability of insights. Implementing a rigorous participant screening process and balancing participant demographics across user groups enhanced the credibility of our research outcomes.

Our final deliverables were well-received by the client, and several of our recommendations were swiftly integrated post-project completion, validating the impact of our research on the design process.
